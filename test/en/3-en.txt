Rediske Air is a small airline operating out of Alaska. The airline is one of hundreds of air charter operators in the state.


== History ==
Rediske Air was formed in 1991. It was started by Charlie Rediske, a local pilot.
In 2001, Charlie Rediske died, and his son Walter and his daughter Lyla became chief operators of the airline.


== Incidents and accidents ==
On July 7, 2013, a Rediske Air DHC-3 Otter plane crashed in Soldotna Airport, Soldotna, Alaska, killing all ten people on board, including Walter Rediske and two families who were on vacation from Greenville, South Carolina.


== Fleet ==
6 De Havilland Canada DHC-3 Otter
